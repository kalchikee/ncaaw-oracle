DEPLOYMENT MODEL: GITHUB ACTIONS + DISCORD EMBEDS
The entire system runs as scheduled GitHub Actions workflows — no dedicated server, no cloud bill. Cron-triggered workflows execute the prediction pipeline on GitHub’s free runners, then send rich embedded messages to Discord via webhooks. Season lifecycle auto-activates in November and goes dormant after the NCAA Championship in April.
WOMEN’S BASKETBALL: UNIQUE MODELING CONSIDERATIONS
WBB differs from men’s in key ways: (1) Greater talent concentration at top programs = more predictable extremes and higher baseline accuracy. (2) Lower scoring (~60–65 PPG vs ~70–75 men’s) and different Four Factors calibration (lower eFG%, higher TOV%, higher ORB%). (3) Star players have outsized individual impact. (4) Less efficient betting markets = more frequent and larger model edges. (5) Less publicly available advanced analytics (no KenPom equivalent) — custom AdjEM computation required. (6) NIL and transfer portal are rapidly increasing parity. CRITICAL: train only on women’s data; do NOT use men’s data.

5. Prior-Year Bootstrap (Early Season)
EARLY SEASON IN WBB
Same challenge as men’s: sparse data early. For each team’s first 5 games, use prior-year adjusted efficiency + recruiting composite + returning minutes + portal WAR. Women’s basketball has historically higher roster continuity (fewer early WNBA departures), so returning minutes is weighted higher than in men’s.
Preseason Rating (Women’s Calibration)
Preseason_Rating = 0.35 × prior_AdjEM + 0.30 × returning_minutes_adj + 0.20 × recruiting_composite + 0.15 × portal_impact
Higher weight on returning minutes (0.30 vs 0.25 for men’s) reflects greater roster continuity. Lower weight on recruiting (0.20 vs 0.25) because women’s recruiting rankings are less predictive.

7. Custom Adjusted Efficiency Metrics
NO KENPOM FOR WBB
There is no direct KenPom equivalent for women’s basketball. Her Hoop Stats provides ratings but may not cover all 364 teams. V4.1 builds custom AdjOE/AdjDE/AdjEM using the same iterative opponent-adjustment methodology as KenPom, applied to women’s box score data from ESPN/NCAA. This runs during the daily workflow as teams accumulate games.
Compute raw OE/DE (points per 100 possessions) for all 364 teams.
Iterate opponent adjustments until convergence (~10 iterations).
Result: AdjOE, AdjDE, AdjEM, AdjTempo calibrated to women’s game.
Alternative: use Her Hoop Stats ratings where available, supplement with custom for remaining teams.
Prior-year custom AdjEM stored in config/prior_year.json for bootstrap.
8. Star Player Dominance Model
MORE IMPACTFUL IN WBB
Individual stars have larger impact in women’s basketball due to greater talent gaps, higher usage rates, and fewer possessions per game. A dominant player can shift win probability by 10–15%. The model explicitly tracks star availability and impact.
Track top player BPM and usage rate for each team.
If star (top BPM on team) is out: apply usage-weighted penalty to team AdjEM.
Freshman phenoms: McDonald’s All-Americans can have immediate massive impact. Track incoming.
WNBA draft impact: top players leaving early increasingly common. Subtract their WAR pre-season.
NIL retention: some programs retain draft-eligible players via NIL. Track retention signals.

11. The Meta-Model: Logistic Regression
P(home_win) = sigmoid(β0 + β1×elo_diff + β2×adj_em_diff + β3×star_player_diff + ...)
Train on: 2018–2024 WBB data ONLY (~33,000 games). Do NOT train on men’s data.
Walk-forward CV. L2 regularization (C=1.0).
Export model_weights.json + calibration_map.json to /model/. Retrain annually.
Simulate prior-year bootstrap in training data.
12. Monte Carlo & Calibration
exp_pts = team_AdjOE × (opp_AdjDE / D1_avg) × expected_poss / 100 × home_adj × rest_adj
10,000 Normal sims. Women’s-specific σ ~11 points per team.
OT: ~7 additional points per team.
Platt scaling: isotonic regression. Especially important in WBB due to more lopsided games.
